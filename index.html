The Beginning | Behala

❤️ The Story

I don’t remember everything from the very beginning, but there is one moment that changed everything for me. It was Rakhi, at nani ghar. I was in 2nd standard — a shy kid who was always a little scared and unsure around people. You had probably met me before, but I was too young to even remember. For me, that day was the first real meeting.

And somehow, in just a few moments, you made me feel comfortable. You didn’t treat me like a small, awkward kid. You became one yourself. You handed me your keypad phone — filled with games that, at that time, felt like the biggest treasure in the world — and just like that, I was happy.

That night, when it was time for you to leave, you casually asked, “Will you come with me to my home?”

I was small, but I still remember looking at Mummy for approval. She agreed… and I went with you. And from that moment on, your home in Ballygunge stopped being just your home. It became mine too.

The Golden Years

Every summer meant only one thing—coming to you.

From class 2 onwards, almost every holiday began the same way. The first day of vacation, my bags would already be packed, and I would rush to Ballygunge like I belonged there. Those 50–60 days didn’t feel like visiting someone. They felt like living where I was meant to be.

Our days were simple, but they meant everything to me. We always ate together. No matter what. Even if I woke up late, I would wait. Even when you had to leave early in the morning for Jetking and come back around 10 or 11, I would sit and wait for you — just so we could have breakfast together. There was something special about that. Even something as simple as bread and chai felt perfect… because it was with you.

At home, I was a non-vegetarian. But at your place, everything was pure veg. And strangely, it never bothered me. I didn’t miss chicken or eggs. Spending time with you mattered more than anything else. In fact, because of you, I started eating everything — karela, patal, vegetables I would have never touched otherwise. And sometimes, just for fun, we would go out and grab a roll together — like a small secret treat.

Evenings had their own magic. We would often go to Birla Mandir, spend time there, and then pick up cakes — black forest, chocolate mousse, and pastries — from Balaram Mullick. And then we would walk back home together. That walk… those evenings… were my favorite part of every vacation.

Mornings were different. Before your daily routine and before your puja, you would sit with me and help me with my studies. You saw potential in me even when I didn’t see it myself. You helped me with my homework, sometimes even writing the headings in your beautiful handwriting. You were always there — for everything I needed help with.

But the biggest thing you gave me was something you never directly taught. You showed me what discipline and devotion look like. I would see you sit in the mandir for hours, praying with full focus. Sometimes I would sit beside you, sometimes I would just wait for you. But slowly, without even realizing it, that part became a part of me too.

And then there was your computer — probably my favorite part of all. You had internet. You knew how to download things. You used torrents, explored movies, found animated and superhero content… and I would just sit beside you, watching everything you did. I didn’t even realize it at that time, but that’s where my interest in tech started. Not from books. From watching you.

You had Cricket 17, and you played it like a kid yourself — hitting centuries, smashing 4s and 6s. Whenever I asked for my turn, you would say my hands were too small to reach the buttons. But honestly… I never really wanted to play. I just wanted to watch you and enjoy that moment.

During IPL, you would bring a big packet of chips, support KKR like anything, and we would enjoy every match like it was a final. And in the evenings, on the terrace, you were the only grown-up playing cricket with all of us kids — and yet, you were the most childlike among us. That was the best part.

"Growing up, I was always a skinny kid. People made fun of me for it. But you… you never cared. You never made me feel less. You accepted me exactly the way I was. And that meant more than I ever said."

The Ideal Role Model

We had our silly moments too. Making random songs about the most ridiculous things… laughing for no reason… just being stupid and happy. There are so many memories like that, I could go on forever.

As I grew older, things started changing. Boards exam came. Studies increased. Vacations became shorter. Visits to Ballygunge became less frequent. And then you moved to Delhi. Distance increased. But somehow… the bond never did.

I still remember how you once kept checking reviews and pictures of a Motorola phone, imagining how it would feel to have it. You saved up, and finally bought it for yourself. And then, years later, when my laptop stopped working and I was struggling… you made sure my studies didn’t suffer. You gifted me a Lenovo laptop. You didn’t just take care of yourself. You always took care of me too.

And now, looking at you today… You are calm. You are respectful. You are deeply religious. You are kind, grounded, and strong. You are everything I look up to. You are my role model.

Time has passed. Life has changed. You got engaged. You got married. And honestly… in my mind, you are still that same person I grew up with. Just… a more mature, complete version of him. And bhabhi — she is truly perfect for you. You both look so right together, like it was always meant to be.

The Truth Is—

I didn’t just grow up with you.
I grew up because of you.
